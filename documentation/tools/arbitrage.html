What is Sports Betting Arbitrage?

Sports betting arbitrage (or "arbing") occurs when different sportsbooks offer odds on the same event that create a mathematical guarantee of profit. By placing bets on all possible outcomes across different bookmakers, you can secure a profit regardless of which outcome occurs.

How Arbitrage Works

When sportsbooks have different opinions on an event's probability, their odds can create gaps in the market. For example:

  • Sportsbook A: Team X to win at +150 odds
  • Sportsbook B: Team Y to win at +180 odds

If the combined implied probability of these odds is less than 100%, an arbitrage opportunity exists. Our calculator determines the exact bet amounts needed to guarantee profit.

Typical Profit Margins

  • 2-4%: Most common arbitrage opportunities across major markets
  • 4-7%: Higher profit margins often found in prop bets and smaller markets
  • 7%+: Rare opportunities usually appearing during line movement or market inefficiencies

Risk Management

While arbitrage betting is mathematically risk-free, there are practical considerations:

  • Odds Changes: Odds can move between placing your first and second bet
  • Bet Limits: Some sportsbooks may limit bet amounts on arbitrage opportunities
  • Account Restrictions: Heavy arbitrage betting may lead to account limitations
  • Void Bets: In rare cases, sportsbooks may void bets due to errors
Important: Always verify odds manually before placing bets. While our system is highly accurate, manual confirmation helps ensure profitable execution.
