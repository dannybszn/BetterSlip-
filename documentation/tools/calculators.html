3. Hedge Calculator

Purpose

Determine optimal hedge bet amounts to guarantee profit or minimize potential losses on existing wagers.

Hedge Strategies

  • Equal Profit: Same profit regardless of outcome
  • Proportional Profit: Profit proportional to odds
  • Break-Even: Minimize losses while preserving upside
  • Partial Hedge: Reduce risk while maintaining profit potential

Common Hedge Scenarios

  • Futures Bets: Hedge championship bets before finals
  • Live Betting: Hedge pre-game bets during games
  • Parlay Insurance: Hedge final legs of parlays
  • Arbitrage Setup: Create risk-free profit scenarios

Example Hedge Calculation

  • Original Bet: $100 on Team A +200 (potential $200 profit)
  • Hedge Bet: $133 on Team B -150 (risk $133 to win $89)
  • Result: Guaranteed profit of $67 regardless of winner
Pro Tip: Use hedging strategically. Don't hedge every bet, but consider it for large payouts, futures bets, or when you've lost confidence in your original wager.

4. Expected Value Calculator

Purpose

Calculate the mathematical expected value of bets to identify profitable opportunities over the long term.

EV Formula

EV = (Probability of Win √ó Profit if Win) - (Probability of Loss √ó Loss if Loss)

Inputs Required

  • Bet Amount: How much you're wagering
  • Odds: Sportsbook odds for the bet
  • True Probability: Your assessment of actual win likelihood
  • Commission/Vig: Sportsbook's built-in edge (optional)

EV Interpretation

  • Positive EV (+EV): Profitable bet over the long term
  • Negative EV (-EV): Unprofitable bet over the long term
  • Zero EV: Break-even bet with no expected profit/loss

Example EV Calculation

  • Bet: $100 on Team X +120
  • Your Assessment: 55% chance Team X wins
  • EV = (0.55 √ó $120) - (0.45 √ó $100) = $66 - $45 = +$21
  • Result: +21% EV bet (excellent value)

6. Kelly Criterion Calculator

Purpose

Calculate optimal bet sizes based on your edge and bankroll to maximize long-term growth while minimizing risk of ruin.

Kelly Formula

f = (bp - q) / b

  • f: Fraction of bankroll to bet
  • b: Odds received (decimal odds - 1)
  • p: Probability of winning
  • q: Probability of losing (1 - p)

Kelly Variations

  • Full Kelly: Maximum growth rate (higher variance)
  • Half Kelly: Reduced risk with 75% of growth rate
  • Quarter Kelly: Conservative approach with lower drawdowns
Important: Kelly Criterion assumes accurate probability estimation. Overestimating your edge can lead to overbetting and significant losses.
