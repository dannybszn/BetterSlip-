What is Promo Conversion?

Promo conversion is the process of converting sportsbook promotional offers (free bets, bonus bets, deposit bonuses) into withdrawable cash. By using hedging strategies across multiple sportsbooks, you can guarantee a profit regardless of the outcome while meeting promotional requirements.

How Promo Conversion Works

üîÑ Basic Free Bet Conversion

Example: Converting a $100 free bet

  1. Find Low Hold Market: Identify betting market with 2% or lower hold
  2. Place Free Bet: Use $100 free bet on one side (+200 odds)
  3. Hedge with Cash: Bet $133 cash on opposite side (-150 odds)
  4. Guaranteed Result: Net $67 cash regardless of outcome

üìä Conversion Efficiency

  • Excellent (75-80%): Using low-hold markets with optimal odds
  • Good (70-75%): Standard markets with reasonable hold
  • Acceptable (65-70%): Higher hold markets when necessary
  • Poor (60-65%): High hold markets (generally avoid)

Free Bet Conversion Strategies

üéØ Simple Conversion (Most Common)

Basic two-way hedge for straightforward conversion:

  • Step 1: Place free bet on one outcome
  • Step 2: Hedge with cash bet on opposite outcome
  • Step 3: Collect guaranteed profit
  • Best For: Standard free bets without restrictions

üîÄ Multi-Way Conversion

Using three-way markets (soccer, hockey with draw) for better efficiency:

  • Advantage: Often better conversion rates
  • Complexity: Requires three separate bets
  • Markets: Soccer draws, hockey 3-way lines
  • Efficiency: Can achieve 80%+ conversion rates

üé≤ Underdog Strategy

Using free bets on big underdogs for maximum value:

  • Theory: Free bets have no risk, so high-odds bets maximize value
  • Risk: Lower conversion efficiency if underdog loses
  • Reward: Full payout if underdog wins
  • Best For: Risk-tolerant bettors seeking maximum upside
Pro Tip: Always use the underdog side for your free bet. Since you don't lose your stake if the free bet loses, you want the maximum payout potential.

Deposit Bonus Conversion

üí≥ Understanding Rollover Requirements

Most deposit bonuses require wagering the bonus amount multiple times:

  • 5x Rollover: Wager bonus amount 5 times (common)
  • 10x Rollover: Wager bonus amount 10 times (standard)
  • 20x+ Rollover: High requirements (evaluate carefully)

Common Promotion Terms

üìã Important Restrictions

  • Minimum Odds: Free bets may require minimum odds (e.g., -200 or longer)
  • Expiration: Time limits on using promotional credits
  • Sport Restrictions: Some promos limited to specific sports
  • Market Exclusions: Certain bet types may be prohibited
  • Maximum Stake: Limits on how much you can bet

‚öñÔ∏è Terms to Watch For

  • "Stake Not Returned": Standard free bet (your stake isn't returned)
  • "Site Credit": Must be wagered, cannot be withdrawn directly
  • "Cash": Actual money that can be withdrawn
  • "Bonus Funds": Usually requires rollover before withdrawal
Important: Always read promotion terms carefully. Missing restrictions can significantly impact conversion efficiency or even void your conversion attempt.
