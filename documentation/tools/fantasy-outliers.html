What are Fantasy Outliers?

Fantasy Outliers are DFS player props where the line differs significantly from sharp sportsbook averages. When a DFS platform has a line that's far off from what sharp books like Pinnacle and Circa are pricing, it creates an opportunity for value betting.

Why Outliers Matter

When DFS platforms price lines differently from sharp sportsbooks, it creates opportunities:

Fantasy Outliers FAQ

What is an outlier in DFS betting?

An outlier is a DFS player prop where the line differs significantly from sharp sportsbook averages. When a DFS platform has a line that's far off from what sharp books are pricing, it creates an opportunity for value betting.

How do you identify outliers?

We compare each DFS line to the average from sharp sportsbooks like Pinnacle, Circa, and Bookmaker. When a DFS line differs significantly (typically 1+ points), it's flagged as an outlier.

What's a good outlier size?

Outliers with 2.0+ point differences offer the most value. However, even 1.0-2.0 point differences can be profitable if the win probability is favorable.

Should I always bet outliers?

Not necessarily. Look for outliers with favorable win probabilities (typically 50%+). A large outlier with a low win probability may still not be worth betting.

How often do outliers appear?

Outliers appear frequently, especially when DFS platforms are slow to adjust lines or when sharp books move their lines. Our platform updates every few seconds to catch new outliers.

Best Practices

  1. Start with Outliers: Check the Outliers tool first to find props where DFS lines differ significantly from sharp projections
  2. Focus on Large Differences: Look for outliers with 2.0+ point differences for maximum value
  3. Check Win Probability: Ensure the outlier has a favorable win probability (50%+) before betting
  4. Act Quickly: Outliers are often short-lived as lines adjust to market consensus
  5. Combine with Builder: Use Outliers to find value, then use Builder to build optimal slips
Important: While outliers can indicate value, they're not guaranteed winners. Always use proper bankroll management and don't bet more than you can afford to lose.
