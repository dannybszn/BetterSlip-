Main Causes of Betting Latency

For arbitrage bettors, understanding the technical hurdles that contribute to delays is essential. These challenges directly impact the speed needed to capitalize on arbitrage opportunities.

Technical Delay Factors

The speed of your network and the physical distance between servers and users play a huge role in latency. In high-stakes situations like major sporting events, even a few milliseconds can mean the difference between placing a winning bet or missing out entirely. For example, traditional TV broadcasts often lag by 30–45 seconds, while streaming delays during events like Super Bowl LVIII have been reported to reach up to 86.75 seconds.

Some of the main technical factors causing delays include:

  • Network Speed: The quality of your connection determines how quickly data is transmitted.
  • Signal Distance: The farther the server is from the user, the longer it takes for data to travel.
  • Processing Time: Internal systems need time to validate bets before they can be placed.
  • Authentication Delays: Security measures, while crucial, can add extra processing steps.

Odds Feed Bottlenecks

Outdated odds collection systems can slow down the delivery of critical data, making it harder to seize arbitrage opportunities. The industry is actively working on technologies that aim for response times under two seconds.

"When you are 15 or 20 seconds delayed it is very easy to miss a bet. If you wait till the last moment to place a bet as many punters like to do, the bet may have closed if the stream is behind, causing a lot of frustration." - Per Lindgren, Net Insight's senior vice president of live OTT
