The Math Behind Arbitrage Betting

To successfully spot arbitrage opportunities, you’ll need to calculate implied probabilities and compare sportsbook odds. Let’s break this down step-by-step.

Converting American to Decimal Odds

First, you’ll need to convert American odds into decimal format. Here’s how:

  • For negative American odds:
    Use the formula: (100 ÷ |odds|) + 1
    Example: For -150, the calculation is (100 ÷ 150) + 1 = 1.67
  • For positive American odds:
    Use the formula: (odds ÷ 100) + 1
    Example: For +200, the calculation is (200 ÷ 100) + 1 = 3.00

Identifying Arbitrage Opportunities

Arbitrage opportunities arise when the combined implied probabilities from different sportsbooks add up to less than 100%.

To calculate implied probability:

  • For decimal odds: (1 ÷ odds) × 100
  • For negative American odds: (|odds| ÷ (|odds| + 100)) × 100
  • For positive American odds: (100 ÷ (odds + 100)) × 100

Here’s a real example from March 2023:

  • Sportsbook A: Over 4.5 goals at +250 (3.5 decimal)
  • Sportsbook B: Under 4.5 goals at -120 (1.8 decimal)

Implied probabilities:

  • Over 4.5: (1 ÷ 3.5) × 100 ≈ 28.57%
  • Under 4.5: (1 ÷ 1.8) × 100 ≈ 55.56%
  • Combined total: 28.57% + 55.56% = 84.13%

Since the total is less than 100%, this is an arbitrage opportunity.

Calculating Optimal Stakes and Profits

Once you’ve identified an arbitrage opportunity, the next step is to calculate how much to stake on each outcome. Use this formula:

Stake = (Total Investment × Implied Probability) ÷ Total Implied Probability

Let’s say you’re investing $1,000 in the example above:

  • Over 4.5 stake: ($1,000 × 28.57%) ÷ 84.13% ≈ $339.54
  • Under 4.5 stake: ($1,000 × 55.56%) ÷ 84.13% ≈ $660.46

Guaranteed profit:

  • If the Over 4.5 bet wins: $339.54 × 3.5 = $1,188.39 (profit ≈ $188.39)
  • If the Under 4.5 bet wins: $660.46 × 1.8 = $1,188.83 (profit ≈ $188.83)

This ensures a profit regardless of the outcome.

Risk Management in Arbitrage

Bankroll Distribution

Managing your bankroll wisely is the cornerstone of successful arbitrage betting. A solid approach involves using a reliable stake calculator and sticking to a consistent staking plan. Here’s how you can do it:

  • Stick to a flat stake of 5–6% of your total bankroll for each arbitrage opportunity.
  • Always keep a portion of your bankroll as a reserve for unexpected situations.
  • Spread your funds across multiple sportsbooks to ensure quick access when opportunities arise.

What are the most common mistakes beginners should avoid in sports betting arbitrage?

When diving into sports betting arbitrage, it’s crucial to steer clear of some common pitfalls that could hurt your profits or jeopardize your accounts. Here are a few key mistakes to avoid:

  • Starting with large stakes: Ease into arbitrage betting with smaller wagers. This helps you understand the process while keeping risks low.
  • Skipping bet rounding: Always round your bets to natural-looking amounts. Odd figures can raise red flags with sportsbooks.
  • Overusing bonuses or promotions: Using too many bonuses can make your account stand out, potentially leading to restrictions.
  • Betting on niche markets: Stick to popular leagues and events. Betting on obscure markets can draw unwanted attention.
  • Frequent withdrawals: Withdrawing funds too often may trigger account reviews, which could disrupt your betting activity.

It’s also wise to avoid betting on glaring odds mistakes and refrain from operating multiple accounts with the same sportsbook. By staying cautious and following these tips, you’ll be better positioned to protect your profits and maintain smooth relationships with sportsbooks.
